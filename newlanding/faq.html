What is Dotball ?

At Dotball, we are designing and developing a comprehensive fan engagement platform for cricket, just for you :). We are the Fantasy Contest Partner for TNPL 2018. Come be a part of the next revolution in cricket. Register on our platform and receive Rs. 51 as cash bonus. Remove the hassle of remembering passwords, login using OTP. Select match, create virtual squad(s) and join contests. It’s as simple as 1,2,3. We offer a plethora of contests to choose from. When the process is scientific, winning is certain. Showcase your talent, your passion for cricket, in this game of skill to earn a lot. Rolling in money is secondary, bragging rights are eternal. Establish yourself as the true champion of Everything Cricket. Your knowledge of cricket and smart thinking are all the tools you need to create a winning virtual squad. You need to draft a team comprising of the virtual counterparts of real-world players and compete against other virtual teams drafted by other participating users. Each virtual counterpart of a real-world player is attributed points based on the statistical elements of the real-world player’s performance in the match(es) in respect of which the fantasy game is offered, whose elements include runs scored, wickets taken, stumpings made, catches taken, sixes scored, strike rate at which runs are scored and runs conceded. The winning user is the one whose virtual team collects the most number of points relative to the other participating users in the fantasy contest.

How are scores updated

The points attributable to each virtual player shall track that player’s match performance in each innings and shall be allocated as follows:

T20 point system

  1. Batting Points
    1. Base Points: 1 point per run.
    2. Pace Bonus Points: A player must score a minimum of ten runs to be awarded the strike rate bonus
      • Strike Rate: 0-74.99: -15 Points
      • Strike Rate: 75-99.99: -10 Points
      • Strike Rate: 100-149.99: +5 Points.
      • Strike Rate: 150-199.99: +10 Points
      • Strike Rate: 200+: +15 Points.
    3. Milestone Bonus: 10 points for scoring 25 runs; 10 points thereafter for each additional score of 25 runs.
    4. Impact Points: +2 points for every six; +1 point for every four; -10 for getting out for a duck (that is, getting out without scoring a run) (not applicable for players categorized as bowlers).
  2. Bowling Points
    1. Base Points: 20 points per wicket taken.
    2. Pace Bonus Points: A player must bowl atleast 1 over to be awarded economy rate bonus
      • Economy: 0-5: +15 Points
      • Economy: 5-8: +10 Points
      • Economy: 8-10: +5 Points
      • Economy: 10-12: -10 Points
      • Economy: 12+: -15 Points
    3. Milestone Points: 10 additional points for taking 2 wickets, 20 additional points for a 3rd wicket and 40 additional points for every wicket thereafter.
    4. Impact Points: 1 point per dot ball bowled. 20 points for each maiden over bowled.
  3. Fielding Points
    1. 10 points for each catch resulting in the fall of a wicket.
    2. 15 points for each stumping resulting in the fall of a wicket.
    3. 10 points for a run-out (to each player involved in the run out).
  4. All points attributed to a virtual player, shall be doubled in case and while such player is declared by the user of that virtual squad as the ‘Captain’ of such squad. All points attributed to a virtual player, shall be multiplied by 1.5 times in case and while such player is declared by the user. The accumulated points of all virtual players in a squad will represent the total points of that squad. This is inclusive of the multipliers due to assigning ‘Captain’ or ‘Vice-captain’ to select players in the team.
  5. Substitutes playing on the field will not be granted point for their contribution. However, Concussion Substitutes (as defined in the Cricket Australia Playing Conditions for Domestic Matches) will be granted points for their contribution, governed by the Cricket Australia Playing Conditions for Domestic Matches.
  6. If a real-life player is transferred in the period between scheduled updates, such a transfer will not be reflected in the list of players until the next update. It’s to be noted that while the transferred player will be available for selection in the transferred squad’s roster of players in a contest during the intervening period, no points will be assigned to that player during that contest.

If two or more users tie for the same place, the winnings are distributed equally among them.

If there is a Super-Over in a match, do I get points for it?

A Super Over isn’t considered in Dotball’s scoring mechanism. Therefore, the run/wickets taken by the players are not counted in our official records.

If a player in an international match is sent off by the umpire for misconduct, does he still score points on Dotball once he’s back on the field?

Yes. The player will continue to score and retain points on Dotball according to our standard point system for his contribution on the field, as per the ICC Playing Conditions for International Matches (effective September 28, 2017). If such player can resume his position on field in the same match, he would be eligible to score points on Dotball.

How are the winnings distributed if there is a tie between the winners of a multiple winner contest?

In case there’s a tie for a position, the winnings for the winner’s and the immediately next position(if any) and so forth in accordance with the number of tied winners, will be collected and equally distributed among the tied winners. Let’s say if there are 2 winners tied for the 1st spot, the winnings for the 1st and 2nd spot will be shared equally between these two winners. And the players scoring the second-highest will get the winnings for the 3rd spot. Currently, there are no tie breakers and consequently, all squads who score equal points are considered as tied for the position.

What happens to the Fantasy match if the ODI/T20 match is cancelled?

In such case, the fantasy match shall be cancelled as well and we’ll refund you your entry fee for the cash contest for both scenarios: The real match is played for a while or when it’s abandoned without any ball play.

Notes:
If the real match ends up in a win to either squad or a tie(but not a ‘no result’ definitely) even if it’s not played to its completion, Dotball will announce the results of the fantasy match according to the accumulated scorecard points. However, there are a few exceptions to this where in a result of a squad is a consequence of:

  • Super Over or Bowl Out
  • Coin toss
  • Prior results or relative standings of the squads in the competition, or
  • Any approach other than the ones foreseen by the official score revision rules(e.g. Duckworth Lewis Stern), related to matches affected by weather in the particular competition.

Where a fantasy match is cancelled for the reasons above or any other reasons, the entry fee of all participants for that specific match will be refunded in its entirety

What happens if a test match is a draw?

If the real test match is declared as a draw with neither squad completing a single inning, the fantasy match will be cancelled and your entry fee will be refunded. Or if the test match is declared as a draw with either squad(and not necessarily both) completing a single inning, the fantasy match will be considered to have completed and the entry fee will not be refunded in this case. To make things clear, an innings will be considered to be completed if a batting squad either declares or get bowled out(loses all ten wickets) in such innings.
